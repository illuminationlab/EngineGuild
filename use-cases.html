Built for every kind of engine shop

Built for how you actually run your shop.

Small engine repair isn’t one business. It’s a mobile mechanic loading a mower into a truck on a Tuesday morning. It’s a storefront shop with twenty units stacked up behind the counter in April. It’s a seasonal specialist who lives on snowblowers October through March. It’s the owner who started solo and now has two techs and a commercial fleet contract. EngineGuild fits all of them — here’s how.

The mobile small engine mechanic

You drive to customers. Your office is the front seat of a pickup.

You fix the mower in the driveway, swap a pull cord on a trimmer on the porch, or diagnose a no-start on a generator in the garage. You can’t answer the phone when you’re under a zero-turn. Calls that go to voicemail walk straight to the next listing.

  • Voice AI picks up every call while your hands are covered in grease. Books the job, confirms the address, and texts the customer a window.
  • Route-day calendars so Tuesdays don’t get stacked with jobs across three counties.
  • Quotes and invoices from your phone, right next to the unit. Customers pay before you pack up the truck.
  • Missed-call text-back catches the ones that slip through. No more $200 jobs walking to the next listing.

The storefront repair shop

The back room has twenty-five units waiting. Which Husqvarna belongs to which customer?

Customers drop off at the counter. The shop stays busy from March through July. Units are waiting on parts, waiting on approval, or just waiting. You’re trying to remember which unit belongs to which job, and the little green tags are falling off.

  • Each drop-off gets an intake record with photos, notes on the complaint, and the unit’s brand and model. Nothing gets lost.
  • Pipeline view shows every unit’s status: diagnosis, waiting on approval, waiting on parts, on the bench, ready for pickup.
  • “Ready for pickup” texts with the total and a pay link. Customer taps, pays, and comes to get it — no third phone call needed.
  • Conversation AI handles the drop-in questions on your website at 9pm so you stop answering “do you service Toro” seventeen times a week.

The seasonal specialist

Snowmobiles, snowblowers, chainsaws. Your calendar runs opposite most shops.

Fall and winter are your peak. Summer is slow and you’d rather it wasn’t. Snowmobile de-winterize in April, full prep in October, and you need customers to book early instead of calling the week of the first snow.

  • Seasonal nurture campaigns pre-scheduled. Text blasts in September for pre-season prep. Email campaigns in March for spring de-winterize.
  • Storage-service membership for snowmobiles and snowblowers, with recurring payment and automated reminder workflows.
  • Review requests fire when the unit is ready — not during the crunch week when you’re fielding forty calls.
  • Off-season content stays on schedule through Content AI and the Social Media Planner. Stay visible in the quiet months.

Commercial fleet and contractor work

Same account, many units, net-30 invoicing. The relationship matters.

You service landscaping companies, grounds crews, marinas, or equipment rental fleets. Standing maintenance schedule. The relationship matters as much as the repair — and your current system can’t hold a fleet’s worth of units under one account.

  • Fleet accounts get a parent record with child records for every unit — mower, blower, trimmer, chainsaw — each with its own service history.
  • Membership portals host contracted maintenance calendars and let crew managers self-serve.
  • Document signing handles fleet service agreements and annual renewals.
  • Automated invoicing supports net-30 terms with polite reminders at day 7, 14, and 21 past due.

The growing shop adding a second tech

You started solo. Now you have one or two techs.

Jobs need to be assigned, routes coordinated, and the owner can’t be the only one who knows what’s going on in the shop. A new tech needs to catch up fast — and shouldn’t have to keep asking you what was promised.

  • Every tech has a login and their own calendar. Assign jobs with one click.
  • White-label mobile app puts every route, customer record, and job detail in the tech’s pocket.
  • Call tracking and recording means a new hire can catch up on what was promised without asking.
  • Per-tech reporting on jobs booked, jobs closed, and average turnaround — you see who’s crushing it and who needs help.
